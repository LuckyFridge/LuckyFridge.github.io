Education Update
by Rudy Portillo
29/Jul/2020

I officially began courses at Western Governors University on July 1, 2020. I am majoring in Cybersecurity and Information Assurance with an expected graduation date of December 2023.

When I first saw that expected date, my heart completely sank. I couldn’t understand how I’ve spent the last ten years in school in some form or another and only had one year worth of credits under my belt. I immediately ran through my transfer evaluation and did some quick napkin math… sure enough… I only transferred enough credits to be considered a sophomore.

I shot off a hasty, yet wordy email to my ‘program mentor’ inquiring as to why I was expected to attend, for a full three years, a school that purported to have ‘accelerated’ programs. Turns out I don’t read the fine print… or listen during orientation… or pay attention in general… because the posted graduation date is a worst-case-scenario deadline for completion. The date is calculated with the supposition that the student is progressing at a snail’s pace, which I am definitely not.

The courses at WGU are structured such that, as soon as you finish one course, you can move immediately to the next one, given you’ve passed the course assessment. Each course has its own study schedule that’s recommended for a timely completion, but moving faster is not only common, but encouraged.

As it stands today, I’m about one month ahead of schedule. If everything goes to plan and my life doesn’t experience any seismic shifts, I should have the degree completed in about eighteen months. It’s all very exciting, as I consider this my THIRD attempt at a bachelor’s degree.

As more time passes, I’m starting to realize that I may be part of the extremely small percentage of people whose lives were actually enhanced by the pandemic. Without the lockdown, I wouldn’t be working from home, and I wouldn’t be able to create the time or environment here at home specifically for study.

I’m tempted to invoke Fate here, but for now I think I’ll chalk it up to being a “glass half full” kinda guy.
