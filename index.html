Another New Cert
by Rudy Portillo
29/Sep/22

As any IT pro will tell you, the world of certifications is vast and annoying to navigate. There are some general certs that can serve as “standard”:

  • CISSP – tells recruiters that you’re willing to subject yourself to pretty much anything to get hired
  • Sec+ – tells recruiters that you had some spare time about 3 months ago and got your company to pay for it to stay in compliance
  • OSCP – tells recruiters that you’re willing to subject yourself to pretty much anything to get hired
  • CEH – tells recruiters that you clicked on the first link when you googled “how to become a hacker”

Then there is a plethora of other tangential certs that really do signal your true skill but are so obscure that you might as well just get your mom to sign them. I recently obtained one of these certs.

Yes, this is the official announcement that I am a CompTIA™ Project+ cert holder.

Scrum? Oh yeah. Cost constraints? You bet ya. Project charter? Yes, plz. Memes aside, I really do think this is a great beginner or “entry-level” project management cert that truly reflects the business aspects of contemporary dev teams. What’s really cool is that I get to put all this new knowledge into action later in the year when I server as interim department chief.

Full disclosure: I had this cert paid for indirectly by my tuition at WGU. It will be up to you if you want to independently pay ~$600 for the vouchers and study guides. Either way, I give this cert a mildly enthusiastic thumbs-up.
