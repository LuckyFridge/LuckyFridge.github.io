New Cert
by Rudy Portillo
11/Oct/2020

I am finally ITIL V4 certified. I also have no idea what that means. Well, I know what it means, but I don’t know what it means. The study content itself reiterated the fact that the ITIL framework can be applied to every single organization, in every single industry, at every single corporate level, and “create value” if implemented properly. Those are some lofty promises and common sense tells me that one-size-fits-all synergistic management solutions are ‘suspect’ to say the least.

The ITIL framework consists of business concepts, that integrate with other business concepts, all wrapped up in a larger, more nebulous business concept. If that seems confusing, that’s because it is. So confusing in fact, that I failed my first attempt at the exam with a 40%. If it wasn’t for the tried-and-true Jason Dion I would have never passed.

That also points out that the coursework provided for the cert by WGU was completely useless. I was passing the ‘practice tests’ with 90s and even scored a 100 on my final practice before the official exam. I was so disheartened by the failure that I took a solid week off school to just play Warzone. With some feedback from other students on the WGU Cybersecurity Discord, I found myself dishing out $12 for Dion training that was guaranteed to get me a passing score. Turns out that 100% of the school’s coursework was only worth about 70% of the actual information I needed.

After going through the convoluted remote exam process, I was issued my gleaming new certification that never expires. I was pretty excited to tell my employer that I had a second cert to show off, but when I told them it was the ITIL V4, they shrugged. It did nothing to elevate my standing in the eyes of the government, so it didn’t even qualify for a mention in the Slack #announcements channel.
